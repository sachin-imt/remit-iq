Two of the most popular ways to send money from Australia to India are **Wise** and **Instarem**. Wise is the transparency champion. Instarem is the speed demon. But when it comes to the number that actually matters — how many Rupees land in your family's account — which one wins?

## How Each Provider Makes Money

Understanding the fee model tells you everything about when each provider shines.

### Wise

Wise gives you the **exact mid-market exchange rate** with zero margin. Instead, they charge an upfront percentage fee (typically around 0.45% for AUD to INR). The total cost is transparent and predictable. There are no surprises hiding in the exchange rate.

### Instarem

Instarem takes the opposite approach. They charge a small flat fee (often $1.99 or less) but build their profit into the **exchange rate margin**, which typically sits around 1% above the mid-market rate. The headline fee looks tiny, but the real cost is baked into the rate.

## Speed: Where Instarem Pulls Ahead

If getting money to India *fast* is your priority, Instarem has a genuine advantage. Their PayID funding combined with UPI delivery can complete a transfer in **under 15 minutes** in many cases. For urgent situations — a medical bill, a tuition deadline, a last-minute festival gift — that speed is worth something.

Wise is no slouch either. Most transfers arrive within minutes to a few hours, depending on your payment method. But Instarem's end-to-end pipeline is optimised for same-day delivery in a way that consistently edges out the competition on speed.

## The Crossover Point: When Each Provider Wins

Here is where it gets interesting. The "best" provider changes depending on how much you are sending.

**For smaller transfers (under $1,500 AUD):**
Instarem's low flat fee can make it competitive. When the send amount is small, Wise's percentage fee takes a bigger bite relative to the total, while Instarem's margin has less room to compound.

**For larger transfers (over $3,000 AUD):**
Wise pulls ahead decisively. Because Wise has zero exchange rate margin, their math scales linearly. On a $5,000 transfer, the difference between a 0% margin and a 1% margin is roughly ₹3,500 — and that gap only widens as the amount grows.

**The sweet spot:**
Between $1,500 and $3,000, the two providers are often neck-and-neck. This is exactly where you should check the live comparison above before deciding.

## Payment Methods Compared

Both Wise and Instarem accept **Bank Transfer** and **PayID** for Australian senders. Wise also supports **Debit Card** funding, which is useful if you want the transfer initiated instantly (though it may carry a slightly higher fee).

For the receiving end, both deliver directly to Indian bank accounts. Instarem also supports delivery via UPI for participating banks, which can speed up the final leg of the transfer.

## Final Verdict

There is no permanent winner here — and that is the whole point. The best provider on Monday might not be the best on Friday.

If you are sending a large amount and want maximum Rupees, **Wise's zero-margin rate almost always wins**. If you need money in India within the hour and you are sending a smaller amount, **Instarem's speed and flat-fee model make it a strong choice**.
