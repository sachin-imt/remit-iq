Australia is home to one of the largest Indian-origin communities in the world, and for many, buying property here is a major life milestone. Whether you're a permanent resident, a citizen, or an NRI with Australian income, navigating the Australian mortgage market requires understanding several key concepts.

## How Australian Mortgage Repayments Work

An Australian mortgage (home loan) works similarly to any amortised loan: you borrow a large sum from a bank or lender, and repay it with interest over a set term — typically **25 to 30 years**.

Your monthly repayment covers:
1. **Interest** — what the bank charges for lending you money
2. **Principal** — the actual debt being repaid

In the early years, most of your repayment goes to interest. Over time, the balance shifts, and the principal reduces faster. This is **amortisation**.

## Key Numbers to Understand Before You Apply

### Deposit (Down Payment)
Australian lenders typically require a **minimum 20% deposit** to avoid Lender's Mortgage Insurance (LMI). With a 10% deposit, LMI can add tens of thousands of dollars to your loan cost.

**Example on a $700,000 property:**
- 20% deposit = $140,000 → No LMI
- 10% deposit = $70,000 → LMI premium of ~$10,000–$15,000

### Interest Rates: Variable vs. Fixed
- **Variable rate:** Moves with the RBA cash rate. Currently competitive, but rises when the RBA tightens monetary policy.
- **Fixed rate:** Locked for 1–5 years. Useful if you expect rates to rise, but less flexible.
- **Split loan:** A portion fixed, a portion variable — the most popular structure for risk-averse buyers.

As of early 2026, variable rates from major banks range from approximately **5.5%–7.0% p.a.** Use our mortgage calculator to model your specific rate.

### Loan Term
Most Australian home loans are structured over **25 or 30 years**. A longer term reduces your monthly payment but significantly increases total interest paid.

**Try in the calculator:** What's the repayment difference between a 25-year and 30-year loan on $600,000 at 6.5%? (Answer: ~$300/month difference, but ~$90,000 more interest over the life of the loan.)

## Additional Costs NRIs and Migrants Often Overlook

### Stamp Duty
This is a state government tax on property purchases, calculated as a percentage of the purchase price. It varies by state and is a significant additional cost.

- **NSW:** On a $700,000 property, stamp duty is ~$26,752
- **VIC:** ~$37,070 on a $700,000 property
- **QLD:** ~$12,850 on a $700,000 property (lower for owner-occupiers)

First-home buyer concessions may apply if you qualify.

### Council Rates and Strata Fees
Budget for ongoing council rates (~$1,500–$3,000/year) and, for apartments, strata fees (body corporate), which can range from $3,000–$15,000+ per year depending on the building.

### Home and Contents Insurance
Mandatory for most lenders. Budget ~$1,500–$3,000/year for a standard home.

## Foreign Buyers: FIRB Approval

If you're not an Australian citizen or permanent resident, you'll need approval from the **Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB)** before purchasing property. There is a FIRB application fee (starting at $14,100 for properties up to $1 million) and foreign buyers are generally restricted to purchasing **new** properties.

For permanent residents and citizens — including those who've recently moved from India — standard mortgage rules apply.
