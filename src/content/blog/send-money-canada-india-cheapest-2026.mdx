Whether you are supporting family back home, paying for a wedding, or handling a property purchase, finding the cheapest transfer service can save you thousands of rupees every year.

This is not a generic overview. These are the specific platforms, fees, and methods that matter for **Canadian senders** in 2026.

## The Four Platforms Dominating the Canada-India Corridor

### 1. Wise — Lowest Total Cost for Most Transfers

Wise consistently delivers the most INR per CAD for transfers above CAD $500. The reason is simple: they charge the **mid-market exchange rate** (zero markup) and apply a transparent fee of roughly **0.45%–0.60%** of the transfer amount. For a CAD $2,000 transfer, that is typically CAD $9–$12 in fees — and every rupee of exchange rate stays in the recipient's account.

Wise accepts funding via **Interac e-Transfer**, which is free for most Canadian bank accounts and settles within minutes. This combination — real exchange rate plus instant, free funding — makes Wise the benchmark for this corridor.

**Delivery to India:** Most transfers via IMPS arrive within 2–4 hours. NEFT transfers complete within the same business day.

**Canada-specific note:** You can also fund via **bill payment** through major Canadian banks (TD, RBC, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC), though this typically takes 1–2 business days to settle.

### 2. Remitly — Best Promotional Rate for New Senders

If you have never used Remitly before, this is arguably the single most valuable tip in this article: **Remitly's first-transfer promotional rate** for new Canadian customers routinely offers an INR rate 1.5%–2.5% above the interbank rate, with fees waived entirely.

On a first transfer of CAD $1,000, that promotion alone can mean an extra ₹1,500–₹2,500 landing in your family's account compared to the standard market rate.

After your first transfer, Remitly's regular rates remain competitive for smaller amounts under CAD $500, particularly on their **Economy** service (3–5 business days, lowest fees) versus their **Express** service (minutes via IMPS, higher margin).

**Accepted Canadian payment methods:** Interac e-Transfer, debit card, credit card (surcharge applies), and bank account (EFT/pre-authorized debit).

### 3. Instarem — Best for Competitive Rates with Rewards

Instarem is a Singapore-founded platform with strong adoption among the Indian-Canadian community. They sit in a middle ground between Wise and Remitly: their exchange rate margin is slightly above Wise's (typically 0.3%–0.8%), but they offset this with an **InstaPoints rewards program** that credits points on every transfer — redeemable for rate reductions on future sends.

For regular senders who transfer every month, the accumulated points can meaningfully reduce the effective cost over time. Instarem also tends to have strong rates during off-peak hours (late evening Canadian time), when liquidity conditions in the INR market are favorable.

**Delivery speed:** IMPS transfers to India typically arrive in under 30 minutes. Instarem's direct banking integrations with HDFC, ICICI, SBI, and Axis are particularly reliable.

**Canada-specific:** Instarem supports **Interac e-Transfer** and direct bank debit. Transfers funded via Interac are typically processed within the hour.

### 4. XE Money Transfer — Best for Large Transfers (CAD $10,000+)

XE (now part of the Euronet group) targets the high-value end of the market. For transfers above CAD $10,000 — property purchases, large family obligations, NRI investments — XE offers **negotiated rates** via their dealing desk that can beat automated platforms.

Their online rates for smaller amounts are not as sharp as Wise or Instarem, but for five- and six-figure transfers, calling XE's Canada desk and requesting a live rate quote is worth the two minutes. There are **no transfer fees** on any XE transfer; their margin sits in the exchange rate itself, typically 0.5%–1.2% depending on volume.

XE also provides forward contracts — useful if you are expecting a large INR payment need in 3–6 months and want to lock in today's CAD/INR rate.

## Canadian Payment Methods: What You Need to Know

| Method | Cost | Speed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interac e-Transfer | Free (most banks) | 30 min–2 hrs | Best option for CAD $10,000 and under |
| Pre-authorized debit (EFT) | Free | 1–3 business days | Good for large or scheduled transfers |
| Debit card | Free–0.5% | Instant | Limited to smaller amounts |
| Credit card | 2.5–3% cash advance | Instant | Expensive — avoid for regular transfers |
| Bill payment | Free | 1–2 business days | Accepted by Wise, XE |

**Interac e-Transfer is the clear default for most senders.** It is free at all Big Five banks and most credit unions, instant, and accepted by every major transfer platform in this corridor. The daily Interac limit at most Canadian banks is CAD $10,000, which covers the vast majority of personal remittances.

## What About Canadian Banks?

TD Bank, RBC, Scotiabank, BMO, and CIBC all offer wire transfers to India. All of them are expensive. Typical costs:
- **Flat fee:** CAD $15–$25 per transfer
- **Exchange rate margin:** 3%–5% above interbank rate
- **Correspondent bank fees:** CAD $10–$20 deducted from the receiving amount in India

On a CAD $3,000 transfer, a Canadian bank can cost you **₹18,000–₹25,000 more** than Wise for the exact same delivery. The only scenario where a bank transfer makes sense is if you are moving very large amounts (CAD $50,000+) and the bank waives fees as part of a wealth management relationship.

## Timing Your Transfer

The CAD/INR rate is most favorable during **overlapping trading hours** — approximately 9am–12pm Eastern Time, when both Canadian financial markets and the Indian foreign exchange market (which closes at 5pm IST, or 6:30am ET) are active. Sending during this window typically yields slightly better rates than late-evening Canadian transfers.

## The Bottom Line

For most Canadian senders:
- **First transfer ever?** Use Remitly's promotional rate — it is unbeatable.
- **Sending CAD $500–$5,000 regularly?** Wise is almost always cheapest.
- **Sending monthly and want rewards?** Instarem's points program adds up.
- **Sending CAD $10,000+?** Get a quote from XE's dealing desk.
