When you're transferring a large sum — say $10,000 AUD or more — to India, even a tiny difference in the exchange rate or a flat fee can mean thousands of rupees lost. Two platforms that consistently compete for large-volume customers are **OFX** and **Wise** (formerly TransferWise).

Both are reputable, regulated, and trusted by hundreds of thousands of Australians. But they are built around very different business models. Here's what actually matters when you're wiring a significant amount.

## How Each Platform Makes Money

Understanding the fee model is the first step to knowing who wins for your transfer size.

**Wise** charges a transparent, upfront **percentage fee** (plus a small fixed fee for bank transfers), but gives you the **mid-market exchange rate** — the real, unbiased interbank rate with zero markup. This model is best-in-class for transparency.

**OFX** takes a different approach: **zero transfer fees**, but they build their margin directly into the exchange rate they offer. For smaller transfers, this spread can cost you more than Wise's flat fee. However, for very large amounts (>$25,000 AUD), OFX's spread becomes more competitive because there's no percentage fee scaling upward.

**Key Rule:** At around $10,000–$15,000 AUD, OFX and Wise tend to reach a cost crossover. Always run both through RemitIQ's live comparison to check the exact breakeven for your specific amount.

## Exchange Rate Markup

When we compare live rates, Wise typically offers the mid-market rate with a **0.37–1.0% fee** applied transparently. OFX's rates carry an implicit margin of around **0.4–0.8%** embedded into the rate itself.

The key difference: with Wise you can clearly see what you're paying. With OFX, you need to compare their offered rate against the mid-market rate (available on RemitIQ) to calculate the true cost.

## Speed and Reliability for Large Transfers

**OFX:** Large transfers often go via the SWIFT network. Expect **1–3 business days** for standard bank-to-bank transfers. For particularly large amounts (>$50,000), OFX assigns you a personal dealer who can help with timing and FX strategy — a significant advantage.

**Wise:** For amounts up to ~$20,000 AUD, Wise is typically **same-day or next business day** for INR transfers. Above that threshold, additional identity verification may add a day.

## Customer Service and Support

This is where OFX clearly differentiates:

- **OFX:** 24/7 phone support, a dedicated account manager for high-value customers, and the ability to lock in forward contracts (agree today's rate for a transfer next month).
- **Wise:** Primarily digital/email support. Excellent for self-service, but less suited to complex or time-sensitive large transfers.

## Bottom Line

| | OFX | Wise |
|---|---|---|
| **Best for** | Large transfers (>$15K AUD) | Small-to-mid transfers |
| **Fee structure** | Rate margin, no flat fee | Flat % fee, mid-market rate |
| **Speed** | 1–3 days | Hours to 1 day |
| **Support** | Phone + dedicated manager | Self-service digital |
| **Forward contracts** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |

If you're sending a large, one-off transfer and value personal service, **OFX** is likely your best option. If you transfer regularly in smaller amounts and want full transparency, **Wise** delivers unmatched value.
