For many Australians sending money to India — supporting parents, paying for a home loan in India, or managing savings across borders — this is a **regular, recurring need.** Not just a one-time transfer, but a financial commitment that happens every month or every few months.

For this use case, **TorFX** and **OFX** are the two services that stand out. Both are built around the idea of serving customers who move money in volume, not just once.

## What TorFX and OFX Have in Common

- Both are FCA-regulated (UK) and comply with all Australian financial regulations
- Both offer dedicated account managers for customers who transfer regularly
- Both support **forward contracts** — locking in today's exchange rate for a transfer you plan to make in the future
- Both charge zero transfer fees and build their margin into the exchange rate
- Both have a minimum transfer amount (typically $2,000 AUD)

These similarities mean the choice often comes down to subtle differences in experience and rate competitiveness on any given day.

## Exchange Rates: Day-to-Day Competition

TorFX and OFX are remarkably close in their rate offers for AUD to INR. On most days, the difference amounts to just a few paise per dollar. Over the course of a year of monthly transfers, however, those paise add up.

## Forward Contracts and Rate Lock-In

Both services offer **forward contracts**, but TorFX has historically been more proactive in suggesting and managing them for customers. If your savings in India or your family's expenses are budgeted in rupees, locking in a rate protects you from the risk of the AUD weakening.

**Example:** If you need to send $2,000 AUD every month for 6 months (total $12,000), you could lock in today's rate across all six transfers — shielding your family from exchange rate risk even if the AUD drops significantly.

## Customer Service and Relationship

Both platforms pride themselves on personal service. Here's the nuance:

- **TorFX** is known for a more "boutique" feel — you often get assigned a named dealer who remembers your transfer history and preferences.
- **OFX** operates at larger scale globally, so the service is excellent but can feel less personal at moderate transfer sizes.

## Which Should You Choose?

| | TorFX | OFX |
|---|---|---|
| **Best for** | Regular monthly transfers | Both regular and one-off large transfers |
| **Rate negotiation** | More flexible for loyal customers | Standard competitive market rates |
| **Forward contracts** | ✅ Proactively offered | ✅ Available on request |
| **Minimum transfer** | $2,000 AUD | $1,000 AUD |
| **Online experience** | More phone/dealer-focused | Stronger self-service web platform |

For someone sending $3,000–$5,000 AUD every month to India, **TorFX's relationship model** often delivers slightly better rates over time because you can negotiate. For larger one-off transfers or if you prefer digital self-service, **OFX** edges ahead.
