## How Each Service Prices a USD → INR Transfer

### Wise: Transparent Fee, Zero Margin

Wise's pricing model on USD to INR has two components:

- **Exchange rate:** The exact mid-market rate. No markup. Zero margin.
- **Fee:** Approximately **0.50% of the send amount**, plus a fixed component of $0.59–$1.50 depending on whether you pay by bank transfer or debit card.

On a $1,000 transfer via bank debit, you're looking at roughly **$5.59–$6.50 total cost**. On $2,000, roughly $10.59–$11.50.

Because the rate margin is zero, Wise's cost scales linearly with the amount. There are no surprises buried in the exchange rate.

### Remitly: Low/Zero Fee, Margin in the Rate

Remitly's model is the inverse. They advertise low or zero upfront fees, but embed their profit in the exchange rate. On the USD to INR corridor in 2026, Remitly's standard rate carries approximately a **0.56% margin** over the mid-market rate on the "Economy" tier (3–5 business day delivery).

On their "Express" tier (minutes to hours), the margin widens — typically **0.9%–1.2%** — because faster processing costs more, and Remitly passes that cost through the rate rather than a visible fee.

**The exception: new-user promotions.** For first-time Remitly users, the promotional rate often beats the mid-market rate outright, with the fee waived entirely. This promo typically applies to your first transfer up to $999–$1,500. If you have never used Remitly before, that promo is worth taking.

## Which One Should You Choose?

### Choose Wise if you're sending $500 or more regularly

Once you're past the first transfer (where Remitly's promo no longer applies), Wise is consistently the better value for amounts above roughly $400–$500. The zero-margin rate compounds: at $3,000, Wise's advantage over Remitly Economy grows to $20–$30. At $5,000, it can reach $40–$60 depending on market conditions.

Wise is also the better choice if you care about **rate transparency**. Because the exchange rate is the true mid-market rate, you can verify exactly what you paid and why. There's nothing hidden.

### Choose Remitly if it's your first transfer

Remitly's new-user promotional rate is one of the best deals in the remittance market — often delivering an effective rate *better* than Wise for the initial transfer. If you've never used Remitly and you're about to send $1,000–$1,500, use the promo, then reassess for subsequent transfers.

### Choose Remitly Express if speed is critical

If the money needs to arrive in India within the hour — a medical situation, a time-sensitive family need — Remitly Express delivers in minutes. Wise's bank transfer option takes 1–2 business days (though Wise's debit card option is also fast). The premium you pay for Remitly Express speed is real but not outrageous on smaller amounts.

## What Both Services Get Right

Neither service is a bad choice. Compared to sending via a US bank SWIFT wire (which typically costs $25–$45 in fees *plus* a 2–3% exchange rate margin), both Wise and Remitly save the average Indian-American family hundreds of dollars a year.

Both are:
- Regulated and compliant with FinCEN and state money transmitter laws
- FDIC pass-through insured on held balances
- Straightforward to set up with standard US bank account or debit card
- Capable of delivering directly to any major Indian bank (HDFC, SBI, ICICI, Axis, etc.)

---

## The Bottom Line

**Wise wins on total value** for most transfers above $500, because a 0% exchange rate margin beats a 0.56% margin even after accounting for Wise's visible fee. The difference is modest at $1,000–$1,500 but becomes material at higher amounts.

**Remitly wins for first-time users** (promo rate) and for cases where same-day or next-hour delivery is genuinely required.
